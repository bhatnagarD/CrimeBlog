The Great Train Robbery (1963)

What Happened: A gang of 15 men hijacked a Royal Mail train in England, stealing £2.6 million (equivalent to about £50 million today). They meticulously planned the heist, using inside information to target a specific train carrying large amounts of cash. Outcome: The gang was eventually caught after leaving behind fingerprints on a Monopoly board at their hideout. Most of the money was never recovered.

The Zodiac Killer (1960s-1970s)

What Happened: The Zodiac Killer was a serial killer who operated in Northern California. He claimed responsibility for several murders and taunted law enforcement by sending cryptic letters and ciphers to newspapers. Outcome: Despite numerous investigations and theories, the identity of the Zodiac Killer remains a mystery to this day.

The Isabella Stewart Gardner Museum Heist (1990)

What Happened: Two men dressed as police officers entered the museum in Boston and stole 13 pieces of art valued at over $500 million. Among the stolen works were paintings by Rembrandt and Vermeer. Outcome: The case remains unsolved, and none of the stolen artworks have been recovered. It is considered the largest art heist in history.

The O.J. Simpson Trial (1994)

What Happened: O.J. Simpson, a former football star and actor, was accused of murdering his ex-wife, Nicole Brown Simpson, and her friend, Ron Goldman. Outcome: After a highly publicized trial, Simpson was acquitted in 1995. The case became one of the most famous and divisive in American legal history.

The Lindbergh Baby Kidnapping (1932)

What Happened: Charles Lindbergh Jr., the 20-month-old son of aviator Charles Lindbergh, was kidnapped from his family’s home in New Jersey. A ransom note demanding $50,000 was left behind. Outcome: The baby was found dead weeks later. Bruno Hauptmann was arrested and executed for the crime, but questions about his guilt persist.

The Ted Bundy Murders (1970s)

What Happened: Ted Bundy was a charismatic serial killer who confessed to murdering over 30 women across multiple states in the 1970s. He used his charm to lure victims, often pretending to be injured or in need of help. Outcome: Bundy was eventually captured, convicted, and executed in 1989. His case is one of the most infamous in U.S. criminal history.

The Brinks Armored Car Robbery (1950)

What Happened: A group of 11 men stole $2.7 million from the Brinks Armored Car depot in Boston, Massachusetts. The robbers left almost no evidence, earning it the nickname "the crime of the century." Outcome: The men were arrested just days before the statute of limitations expired. Most of the stolen money was never recovered.

The Enron Scandal (2001)

What Happened: Enron, once a leading energy company, collapsed after it was revealed that executives used accounting loopholes to hide billions in debt and inflate profits. Outcome: The scandal led to criminal charges for top executives and the bankruptcy of the company. It also resulted in stricter regulations for corporate governance and accounting.
